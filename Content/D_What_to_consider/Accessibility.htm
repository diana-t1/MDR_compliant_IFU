Accessibility

Ensuring IFU are accessible is about more than just language—it’s about making sure every user, whether a healthcare professional or a patient, can easily understand and apply the information provided. Under the Medical Device Regulation (MDR 2017/745), accessibility is a key requirement, ensuring that IFU are clear, well-structured, and available in formats that suit different users and environments.

Aspect Key considerations
Format flexibility MDR encourages electronic IFU (e-IFU). e-IFU reduces paper waste, ensures access to updated instructions, and improves searchability.
Accessibility for visual impairments If the device is intended for use by persons with visual impairments, manufacturers should ensure that web-based IFU meet accessibility standards, such as WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines), to improve usability for those relying on assistive technologies.
Usability & structure IFU must be logically organized, using headings, bullet points, and visual aids (e.g., diagrams) to improve comprehension. Simplified language is essential for layperson users, while standardized symbols help with quick recognition.
Physical usability For devices used in clinical settings, IFU should be designed for quick reference, ensuring that healthcare professionals can easily locate critical information during procedures. For implantable or reusable devices, IFU must provide clear guidance on cleaning, sterilization, and safe reuse, ensuring patient safety.
